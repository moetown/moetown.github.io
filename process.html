Process

A general guide to tools, methods and ways of working

01.

Discovery

The discovery phase for me is about untangling a customer problem and it's assumptions into verifiable hypotheses which can be aired with customers.


Methods:

Internal workshops, surveys, semi-structured interviews, focus groups, presentations


Tools:

Whiteboard, Miro, Intercom, Zoom, EnjoyHQ, Post-its.




The problem to solve

A crucial part of the discovery is to understand the problem and the job the customer is trying to do.

Running workshops, interviews, observation studies, and listen-ins are a crucial part of my role. To understand what the problems are, where in the customers workflow they occur and what their most annoying painpoints are.

Our users

Synthesising our user insights into user journeys, personas and other segmented representable visuals is a way to better understand where people are truing to achieve and what they do to get there. I've used Enjoy HQ to debrief on interviews and to synthezie insights into presentable content, as well as Miro where most of our collaborative work happens in the team.

Having the team involved in these steps and engaged in the research is a pre-requisite for it to be successful.

02.

Ideation

Next step would be to work through the research and bring it into hypothetical solutions for how we might help improve the job the customer is trying to do. The whiteboard is a designers and its teams best friend! (Or Miro, when working remote). The output would be a hypothetical design for POC in a very lo-fi format, which could be refined further to run a concept test with a set group of target users.



Methods:

Design sprint, sketching, wireframing, prototyping


Tools:

Whiteboard, post-its, Miro, Sketch, Figma, Invision, Zoom



Sketching

With feedback from customers and interal stakeholders the first sketches can be ideated within the team. For me, this is typically the designer, PO, dev and maybe tester who'll come together and share experiences!

This is one of the most fun activites when done with shared openness and curiousity!

Concept

As the experience is refined in the team, the concept will start to emerge and can be iterated on throughout concept testing with customers.

My role is to run the tests with mockups, prototypes, as well as share the insights with the team and iterate.

03.

Testing

Useful ways of quickly validating the design concept would be through using a prototype in Invision or Figma. I'd typically interview the user at the same time and ask them to walk me through their process and thinking, while observing their interactions.




Methods:

Concept testing, Usability testing, Surveys, Internal presentations


Tools:

Figma, Invision, Zoom



Concept & usability test

I have in previous projects used an interactive prototype whilst observing the interactions - either in the same room or on remote. The scope of the project will determine how thourough you need to be here, but obviously any facial expressions and interactions are easier observed irl. At Avinode, we do all our testing remote due to having our custumers distributed all around the world. Usability testing is generally done at a later stage in the project, when there's a refined design.

Any feedback gathered can be summarized in a test report to build insights from the testing, as well as overall insights from all tests. I always ask to record the session to be able to debrief thouroughly afterwards. EnjoyHQ is a tool I've used for research over the the last year which helps with transcribing videos and audio into text as well as segmenting data into different topics through its great functionality for taxonomy and rules.

04.

Release & track

A validated design will eventually reach development where the developers will get access to mockups, prototypes and descriptions of how the design is intended to be built. As its released, it's key to follow up on key metrics to ensure the design is fulfilling to the customers needs



Methods:

Story mapping, development reviews, Beta test validation


Tools:

Jira, Test-server, Intercom, Google Analytics



Design delivery

In this part of the project I'll typically share the design in Figma, Invision or Abstract with a detailed description of it in Jira.

Working closely with developers and testers is in this part of the project so much fun, and you deliver the end solution together as a team. Following up on the release with qualitative and quantitative data in interviews, surveys and GA is key to ensure that the design is on target.
