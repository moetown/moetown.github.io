Project start

The initial roadmap is crucial for our products success

Every new project has variables. Timeframes, requests, visual requirements, stakeholders, style and much much more. I try to keep it simple and just listen and ask questions.

My job is to make your vision come true and at the same time combine that with the user's ultimate experience. I will do my utter most to make sure that the user's needs are always in the spotlight. Its therefor crucial that we both take the time to get to know what it is we're going to do, how its going to happen and when. We'll create a roadmap. This has historically taken form of a Gantt chart or a project in Asana or Trello.

Early ideation

some examples of tools and methods

The teams I've been working with before has typically mapped out ideas on a whiteboard in all phases of the project. There's so many benefits to this, as all involved can be part of the creation and give their input as to what's going to happen. Early architecture, wireframes, timelines - all can be easily modified and created. If not a whiteboard, we'd typically use big A1 papers to do the same exercice. This can also be turned into post-it brainstorming sessions, De Bonos thinking hats, KJ analysis etc.

Next, we'd like to test if our hypothesises are true with real users as it is, unless investigated, just guesses. So this is where running workshops, interviews, observation studies and listen ins become really crucial.

01.

Brief

What are we going to do and how, when, why and perhaps even where if that's a variable. Let's put it all in Trello or Asana together piece by piece and see where we land. if there's things we already know about the product this is a good time to put those in as requirements.

02.

Hypothesis

What are the ideas we want to test out? Who are our users? Whiteboard wireframes, sketches and brainstorm around these. Target user group and personas are defined. If we have an existing product we could look at Analytics/ Hotjar for guidance.

03.

User validation

For an existing product - mockups with A/B test and analyse data through observation and/or heatmaps. Always with the right personas/user group. For a new product it might be a good idea to run a workshop and/or interviews to get qualitative data.
