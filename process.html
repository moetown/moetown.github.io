The project

The initial roadmap is crucial for our product's success

Every new project has variables. Timeframes, requests, visual requirements, stakeholders, style and much more. I try to keep it simple and just listen and ask questions initially.

My job is to make your vision come true meanwhile combining that with the user's ultimate experience. It involves a lot of communication and collaboration, ideation, and quick problem solving. As a multi-tasker, this is perhaps one of my more refined skills: keeping a lot of things going at once.

Discovery work

Problem solving at its finest

The first thing to figure out is what problem to solve, because there might be many, and how do we know what the users really consider is the most prominent problem? Who are even our users?

This is where running workshops, interviews, observation studies, and listen-ins become crucial. Examples from previous work can be found in the section about my thesis work with Ericsson & Semcon.

01.

Research

In order to set requirements, we need to know who those requirements are for and why they exist. So at this point the target users should be well understood and user needs highlighted and prioritized in user journeys and pesonas. Otherwise, we might risk getting biased to our own ideas of what to solve for.


02.

Ideation

Brainstorm with architectural approach, sketches, and whiteboard wireframes.

Early architecture, wireframes, and POCs - all can be easily created and modified together with the team in an iterative process. If not a whiteboard, we'd typically use big A1 papers to do the same exercice.

03.

Validation

Useful ways of quickly validating our hypothesis would be through either using paper mockups or a digital prototype in Invision with Usability testing. I'd typically interview the user at the same time and ask them to walk me through their process, and observe their interactions.

Persona evaluation done at Ericsson & Semcon

Have we solved it?

The next step in the process is to analyse the result. Did we succeed with solving for the user problems? Was it a pretty smooth worflow for the user? Did we meet our expectations for the project? If yes, we should be quite confident that we can continue to develop our ideas in code. We can later follow up on our success metrics through using google analytics or hotjar.
