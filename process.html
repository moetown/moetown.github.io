My process

A general guide to tools, methods and ways of working

01.

Discovery

The first step of every project I've been part of has involved customer research. From a user experience perspective, it's all about understanding the users workflow, their needs in the given context, pain points they might encounter.


Methods:

Internal workshops, surveys, semi-structured interviews, focus groups, presentations


Tools:

Whiteboard, Miro, SurveyMonkey, Appcues, Zoom, Lookback.io, EnjoyHQ, post-its.




The problem to solve

A crucial part of discovery is to figure out is what problem to solve, because there might be many, and how do we know what the users really consider is the most prominent problem?

This is where running workshops, interviews, observation studies, and listen-ins becomes a crucial part of my role. To understand what the problems are, where they occur and what the most annoying painpoints are. I've runned interviews both in person and on remote. Lookback and Zoom has been helpful for me to run and sometimes record the sessions.

Our users

Synthesising our user insights into user journeys, personas and other segmented representable visuals is a way to better understand where people are coming from and where they're trying to go. I've used Enjoy HQ to debrief on interviews and to synthezie insights into presentable content, as well as Miro where most of our collaborative work happens in the team.

Having the team and stakeholders involved in these steps and engaged in the research outcome is crucial for the next steps of the design work. Sharing through research presenations is here very helpful.

02.

Ideation

Next step would be a brainstorming session with the team to map out flows, ideas and journeys based on the research insights. The whiteboard is a designers best friend! (Or Miro, when working remote). The output would be a hypothetical design for POC.



Methods:

Internal workshops, concept testing, prototyping, wireframing


Tools:

Whiteboard, post-its, Miro, Sketch, Figma, Invision, Zoom, Lookback.



Team collaboration

This is the most fun and exploratory part where a lot of different brilliant minds come together to solve a shared problem. The more open the culture is in the company, the easier it is to build on eachothers ideas and create great concepts.

The concepts should be shared with the customers as soon as possible to get feedback on the overall feasability, what specifically works well, and what they consider worth continuing with. This POC will lead the way for the next steps ahead!

Designing the skinny version

This part of the project could be done in a smaller team consisting of UX, Dev and PO for instance with the feedback from the team. This is where POC morphs into a more refined UI.

My role here is to get a more refined design ready for validation through Usability testing. This MVP should be close to the finish line and be as pixel perfect as possible. Using a design library helps to put this together quicker, and getting a consistent UI with the rest of the product.

03.

User validation

Useful ways of quickly validating the design would be through using a prototype in Invision or Figma mirror app for mobile. I'd typically interview the user at the same time and ask them to walk me through their process and thinking, while observing their interactions, and aks questions.



Methods:

Usability testing, Internal presentations, Surveys


Tools:

Lookback, Zoom, Figma mirror, Enjoy HQ



Testing the experience with our users

I have in previous projects used an interactive prototype whilst observing the interactions - either in the same room or on remote. The scope of the project will determine how thourough you need to be here, but obviously any facial expressions and interactions are easier observed irl. At Avinode, we do all our testing remote due to having our custumers distributed all around the world.

Any feedback gathered can be summarized in a test report to build insights from the testing, as well as overall insights from all tests. I always ask to record the session to be able to debrief thouroughly afterwards, and to avoid confirmation bias. EnjoyHQ is an excellent tool for this - transcribing videos and audio into text as well as segmenting data into different topics through its great functionality for taxonomy and rules.

04.

Design & build

Any feedback given during the tests will lead to possible iterations of the final UI. Releasing it in Beta to a set group of target users has been my teams way of handing it, making sure that any potential tweaks are encountered in a larger group setting.



Methods:

Story mapping, development reviews, Beta tester validation


Tools:

Test-server with developed designs, Appcues



Design delivery

In this part of the project I'll typically share the design in Abstract to our Jira tickets for the specific part of the development process.

Working closely with developers and testers is in this part of the project so much fun, and you deliver the end solution together as a team. When released in beta, we follow up with a survey to those users to make sure that we're on target.
